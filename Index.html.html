Memory Architecture
The Context Drive
The first memory system
that thinks like a brain
80
Years of memory. 256GB.
The human brain stores roughly 2.5 petabytes of information. Our CXF compression format stores 80 years of your daily life in 256GB — by encoding meaning, not data. A 2,000-word conversation compresses to 40 words. What survives is not what was said. It is what it meant.
MCA
Actively decides what to remember.
Every night while you sleep, the Memory Consolidation Agent runs silently on the Context Drive. It scores every memory across five dimensions — emotional intensity, relationship proximity, how often you've recalled it, whether it led to a decision, and whether you flagged it as important. Then it keeps, compresses, or permanently erases. Just like your hippocampus does during deep sleep.
Score 0.8+
Core memory — kept verbatim forever. Your father's call. The job offer. The decision that changed everything.
Score 0.5+
Notable — compressed to its meaning. A work call becomes three lines. The essence survives. The noise disappears.
Score 0.2+
Pattern — merged into a cluster. Fourteen morning commutes become one pattern: "leaves at 8am, calm, by car."
Below 0.2
Erased. Permanently. Not archived, not hidden — cryptographically wiped. The first AI with a genuine right to forget.
"The soul layer."
After months of MCA passes, a crystallised identity file emerges — T5, the soul layer. 500MB that captures not what happened to you, but who those things made you. Even if every conversation were deleted, ARIA would still know you. That file is stored only on your Context Drive. No company can read it. No server holds a copy. It is yours.
